What is Tetris

Tetris is a classic tile matching video game created in 1984 where geometric shapes composed of four square blocks known as tetriminos fall from the top of the playing field of which there are 7 different types. Players must rotate and move these blocks to complete a line. When a line is complete it disappears and any blocks above get shifted down. The game ends when the stack of tetriminos reaches the top of the playing field. Essentially more lines you clear the bigger your score.

Our model will look to reward good moves and penalise bad ones.

What are good and bad moves

Good moves include completing lines specifically more than one at a time, minimising the risk of creating a hole.

A good move that would be considered advanced specifically for an AI would be setting up the ability to clear multiple lines such as leaving a gap for a line piece so as to complete a tetris, when you clear four lines at once, this also rewards you with the most amount of points.

On the other hand bad moves are ones that create gaps and not rotating for optimal block placement.
